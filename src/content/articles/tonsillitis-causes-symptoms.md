# Tonsillitis: Causes, Symptoms & When to Seek ENT Care

Are you or your child suffering from repeated sore throats or severe throat pain? Tonsillitis is a common condition, but when it becomes recurrent, it can significantly disrupt your daily life, school attendance, and work. If you are in Entebbe or the wider Uganda region, it is crucial to distinguish between a simple viral sore throat and bacterial tonsillitis that requires professional medical care. At Eritage ENT Care in Entebbe, we specialize in accurate diagnosis and effective management of both acute and recurrent tonsillitis to prevent complications.

## What causes tonsillitis?

Tonsillitis is inflammation of the tonsils, the two immune-system glands at the back of your throat. It can be caused by viruses (the most common cause) or bacteria.

### Common causes include:
- **Viruses:** Adenovirus, Rhinovirus, Influenza, and Epstein-Barr virus (Glandular Fever). These do not respond to antibiotics.
- **Bacteria:** Group A Streptococcus (Strep A) is the most critical cause. Untreated Strep throat can lead to serious complications like rheumatic heart disease, which remains a significant health concern in Uganda.

## When to see an ENT specialist in Entebbe

Do not ignore throat pain if it is severe or persistent. Seek professional evaluation at Eritage ENT Care if you or your child experience:

- **Frequent Infections:** 5 or more episodes of tonsillitis per year.
- **Severe Symptoms:** Inability to open the mouth (trismus), severe one-sided pain, or a "hot potato" muffled voice—these can indicate a peritonsillar abscess.
- **Breathing/Swallowing Issues:** Audible stridor (harsh breathing sounds), difficulty breathing, or uncontrollable drooling.
- **Non-resolving pain:** A sore throat that lasts longer than 3 weeks or is rapidly worsening.
- **Neck Lumps:** New or persistent lumps in the neck.

**If you have difficulty breathing or swallowing saliva, seek emergency medical attention immediately.**

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is my sore throat tonsillitis?
Tonsillitis is specifically inflammation of the tonsils, often visible as redness or white patches. Many sore throats are pharyngitis (general throat inflammation). An ENT specialist can make the distinction.

### Does my child need their tonsils removed?
If your child has recurrent episodes (e.g., 5-7 times a year) or if their tonsils are large enough to cause breathing/sleep apnea, an ENT specialist may recommend a tonsillectomy to improve their quality of life.

### Why is Strep throat dangerous?
Untreated Strep throat can lead to rheumatic fever, which can cause permanent damage to the heart valves. Proper diagnosis and a full course of antibiotics are essential for prevention.
